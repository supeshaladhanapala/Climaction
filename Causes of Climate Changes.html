    1. Human-Induced Climate Change:


    The primary driver of recent climate change is the increase in greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions, largely from human activities. The burning of fossil fuels (coal, oil, and natural gas), deforestation, and certain industrial processes release GHGs such as carbon dioxide (CO2) and methane into the atmosphere.

    2. Greenhouse Effect:


    Greenhouse gases trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere, creating a natural greenhouse effect that keeps the planet warm enough to support life. However, the enhanced greenhouse effect caused by increased GHGs contributes to global warming.

    3. Global Warming:


    Global warming refers to the long-term increase in Earth's average surface temperature. The Earth has experienced periods of warming and cooling throughout its history, but the current warming trend is occurring at an accelerated rate.

    4. Impacts on Weather Patterns:


    Climate change leads to changes in weather patterns, including more frequent and severe heatwaves, altered precipitation patterns, increased intensity of storms, and changes in the frequency of extreme weather events.

    5. Rising Sea Levels:


    The melting of glaciers and polar ice caps, along with the expansion of seawater as it warms, contributes to rising sea levels. This poses risks to coastal areas and low-lying regions.

    6. Ecosystem Disruptions:


    Climate change affects ecosystems and biodiversity, leading to shifts in habitats, changes in migration patterns, and increased risks of extinction for some species.

    7. Ocean Acidification:


    Increased CO2 levels in the atmosphere also lead to higher CO2 absorption by the oceans, causing ocean acidification. This can harm marine life, particularly organisms with calcium carbonate shells and skeletons.

    8. International Agreements:


    The global community recognizes the need for collective action to address climate change. The Paris Agreement, adopted in 2015, is a landmark international accord aimed at limiting global warming and promoting climate resilience.

    9. Mitigation and Adaptation:


    Mitigation involves reducing or preventing the emission of GHGs, while adaptation involves making adjustments to social, economic, and environmental practices to minimize the impacts of climate change.
