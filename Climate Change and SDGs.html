    SDG 1:
    No Poverty

    Climate change exacerbates poverty by affecting agricultural productivity, water resources, and increasing the frequency of extreme weather events.

    SDG 2:
    Zero Hunger

    Changes in temperature and precipitation patterns disrupt agricultural systems, leading to food insecurity and challenges in achieving food production goals.

    SDG 3:
    Good Health and Well-being

    Climate change can influence the spread of infectious diseases, alter the distribution of vector-borne diseases, and contribute to health risks associated with heatwaves and extreme weather events.

    SDG 6:
    Clean Water and Sanitation

    Changes in precipitation patterns can lead to altered water availability, affecting both quantity and quality. Increased temperatures can exacerbate water scarcity in some regions.

    SDG 7:
    Affordable and Clean Energy

    Climate change mitigation efforts often involve transitioning to clean energy sources, reducing reliance on fossil fuels. At the same time, changes in climate patterns can impact energy infrastructure and supply.

    SDG 11:
    Sustainable Cities and Communities

    Climate change can affect urban areas through increased heat, changes in precipitation patterns leading to flooding, and the potential for increased frequency of extreme weather events, impacting infrastructure and communities.

    SDG 13:
    Climate Action

    Climate change is both a consequence and a driver of the need for climate action. Adapting to climate change and mitigating its impacts are critical components of achieving SDG 13.

    SDG 14:
    Life Below Water

    Changes in sea temperature, ocean acidification, and sea-level rise are consequences of climate change, affecting marine ecosystems and biodiversity.

    SDG 15:
    Life on Land

    Climate change requires collaborative efforts and partnerships to address its impacts. Sharing knowledge, technology, and resources is crucial for effective climate action and achieving sustainable development.

    SDG 17:
    Partnerships for the Goals

    Climate change requires collaborative efforts and partnerships to address its impacts. Sharing knowledge, technology, and resources is crucial for effective climate action and achieving sustainable development.
