    1. Rising Temperatures

    - Heatwaves: Increased frequency and intensity of heatwaves, leading to health risks, heat-related illnesses, and stress on ecosystems.

    2. Changes in Precipitation Patterns:

    - Droughts: Prolonged and severe droughts in some regions, affecting water availability for agriculture, industry, and communities. - Floods: Increased risk of heavy rainfall and flooding in certain areas, causing damage to infrastructure and displacement of communities.

    3. Sea Level Rise:

    - Coastal Erosion: Rising sea levels contribute to coastal erosion, threatening infrastructure and displacing communities. - Saltwater Intrusion: Infiltration of saltwater into freshwater sources, impacting agriculture and drinking water supplies.

    4. Extreme Weather Events:

    - Hurricanes and Cyclones: Increased frequency and intensity of tropical storms, hurricanes, and cyclones, posing risks to coastal areas. - Wildfires: More frequent and severe wildfires due to higher temperatures and changes in precipitation patterns.

    5. Melting Ice and Glaciers:

    - Arctic and Antarctic Ice Loss: Melting ice contributes to rising sea levels and affects ecosystems and wildlife in polar regions. - Glacier Retreat: Reduction in the size and volume of glaciers, impacting water resources for downstream communities.

    6. Ocean Acidification:

    - Increased absorption of CO2 by the oceans leads to ocean acidification, affecting marine life with calcium carbonate shells and skeletons, such as corals and certain shellfish.

    7. Ecosystem Disruptions:

    - Shifts in Habitats: Changes in temperature and precipitation patterns can alter the distribution of plant and animal species, affecting biodiversity. - Species Extinction: Increased risk of extinction for some species that are unable to adapt to changing conditions.

    8. Impacts on Agriculture:

    - Crop Failures: Changes in temperature, precipitation, and the frequency of extreme weather events can lead to crop failures and reduced yields. - Shifts in Growing Seasons: Changes in growing seasons and the suitability of certain regions for agriculture.

    9. Health Risks:

    - Vector-Borne Diseases: Changes in climate patterns can affect the distribution of disease-carrying vectors (such as mosquitoes and ticks), impacting the spread of diseases like malaria and Lyme disease. - Heat-Related Illnesses: Increased risk of heat-related illnesses and mortality during heatwaves.

    10. Social and Economic Disruptions:

    - Displacement and Migration: Climate-induced disasters can lead to population displacement and migration. - Economic Losses: Damages to infrastructure, agriculture, and industries can result in economic losses and increased social vulnerability.

    11. Threats to Food Security:

    - Changes in climate can disrupt food production, distribution, and access, posing risks to global food security.

    12. Biodiversity Loss:

    -Climate change poses a significant threat to global biodiversity. Alterations in temperature, precipitation patterns, and ecosystems disrupt the habitats of many plant and animal species. As a result, some species may struggle to adapt and face an increased risk of extinction.
