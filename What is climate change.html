    What is Climate Change?

    Climate change refers to long-term and significant alterations in the Earth's climate patterns, particularly those related to temperature, precipitation, and weather events. The term is commonly used to describe changes observed over decades to millions of years. In contemporary discussions, "climate change" often refers to the changes occurring since the mid-20th century and is largely driven by human activities.
